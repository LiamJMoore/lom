# NDA Review Playbook

Use these positions when triaging an inbound NDA. For each clause that violates a rule,
flag it. For each missing-but-required clause, flag it as a gap.

## Hard requirements (sign-blockers if violated)

1. **Mutuality**: The NDA must be mutual (both parties bound). Reject one-way NDAs unless we are the disclosing party only.
2. **Term**: Confidentiality obligations must terminate within 3 years of disclosure. Reject indefinite or "in perpetuity" terms unless the info is genuinely trade-secret.
3. **Governing law**: Must be England & Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland. Reject US state law, UAE, or anywhere requiring local counsel.
4. **No IP assignment**: NDA must not assign or licence any IP, inventions, or work product. NDAs are for confidentiality only.
5. **No non-compete / non-solicit**: NDA must not contain restrictive covenants on employment or business activity.
6. **Definition of Confidential Information**: Must include standard carve-outs — (a) already known, (b) publicly available, (c) independently developed, (d) lawfully received from a third party.

## Negotiable (flag but not a blocker)

7. **Injunctive relief language**: Acceptable if reciprocal; flag if one-sided.
8. **Return/destruction of materials**: Should allow electronic backup retention for legal/compliance.
9. **Residual knowledge clause**: Preferred but not required.
10. **Jurisdiction**: Prefer exclusive jurisdiction of English courts; non-exclusive is acceptable.

## Severity scale

- `BLOCKER` — do not sign without amendment (rules 1–6)
- `NEGOTIATE` — request change but signable if pushed (rules 7–10)
- `NOTE` — drafting issue or minor risk, FYI only
